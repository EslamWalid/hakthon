Suggested apps for Autism see more >>

Autism Therapy with MITA

Developed by three Ivy League researchers, Autism Therapy with MITA is an app designed to help children with autism learn using pivotal response treatment, a type of therapy for autistic children.

Sesame Street and Autism

Everyone knows “Sesame Street.” But did you know the iconic television show has also developed an autism app? This app is as much for parents as it’s for children.

Autism Read & Write

From social exchanges to traditional education, the experiences of children on the spectrum are unique. Autism Read & Write attempts to tackle one small area of their journey: reading and writing.

ABC Kids - Tracing & Phonics

Tracing & Phonics from ABC Kids wasn’t specifically designed for autistic children, but like many others on our list, it’s completely appropriate and could prove fun and educational.

Suggested apps for Sigth problems see more >>

LookTel

LookTel Money Reader instantly recognizes currency and speaks the denomination, enabling people experiencing visual impairments or blindness to quickly and easily identify and count bills

KNFB Reader App

The KNFB Reader converts printed text into high-quality speech to provide accurate, fast, and efficient access to both single and multiple page documents with the tap of a button on the iPhone

Color ID Free

Discovers the Names of the Colors Around You Color ID Free uses the camera on your iPhone to speak the names of colors in real-time

Be My Eye

The One Everyone’s Been Talking About- People Helping People in Real-Time Be My Eyes – Be the eyes for a blind person in need of help remotely through a live video connection if you are sighted or be assisted by the network of sighted users if you are blind

Suggested apps for physical issues see more >>

Assistive Touch

For some smartphone users with physical disabilities, even the apparently simple task of operating the mobile can be challenging because it is difficult for them to perform gestures with their fingers on the touch-screen or to use the physical buttons of the device.

IFTTT

With this app, it is possible to connect all your internet services and social networks, so tasks are automatically completed. There are numerous ways to do this, and the resulting combinations are called “Applets”. They essentially automate your daily workflow.

Wheelmate

Wheelmate is an app dedicated to locating accessible toilet facilities and parking spaces for wheelchair users when they are in the move. With over 30,000 locations across 45 different countries, you’re sure to find the information you need.

Mouse4all Switch

Mouse4all Switch allows to use an Android device entirely without touching the screen. Persons with a severe physical disability can enjoy mobile technology controlling a mouse pointer with one or two switches. This app is very easy to operate.

Suggested apps for Hearing issues see more >>

AVA

Lip reading can be harder in a group of people and this is one of the main reasons AVA was created. If a person who is deaf or who has hearing loss is with a group of friends, they can get those friends to connect to the app.

Rogervoice

Rogervoice is an app which produces live transcription during phone calls in more than 100 different languages. People who are deaf and those who have hearing loss, or someone who has difficulty speaking can use the phone to have a conversation with someone.

Voxsci

Voxsci is a speech-to-text app which translates voicemail messages into texts and emails which can be saved, searched and shared. Costs start at £5 a month for 30 voicemail texts or emails.

Braci Sound Alert

Braci Sound Alert app lets you record the sounds in your environment and then gives you visual and vibrational alerts on your smartphone when it recognises them. For example, it can alert you when an alarm goes off or when a doorbell rings.
